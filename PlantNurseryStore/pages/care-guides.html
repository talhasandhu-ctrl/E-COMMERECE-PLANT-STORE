Plant Care Guides

Keep your plants healthy and thriving with our expert tips on watering, sunlight, soil, fertilizing, and repotting.

Watering

Watering is the most crucial part of plant care. Always check the top 2 inches of soil before watering. If it's dry, water thoroughly until it drains out the bottom. Succulents need less water, while tropical plants prefer slightly moist soil.

Sunlight

Most indoor plants thrive in bright, indirect sunlight. Direct sun can burn leaves, while too little light can slow growth. Observe your plant's leaves: pale leaves often mean too little light, while brown spots can mean sunburn.

Soil

Use well-draining soil to prevent root rot. A standard potting mix works for most plants, but succulents and cacti require a sandy mix. A good mix retains moisture but allows excess water to escape quickly.

Fertilizing

Feed your plants during the growing season (spring and summer) every 2-4 weeks with a balanced liquid fertilizer. Do not fertilize during winter when the plant is dormant.

Repotting

Repot your plants every 1-2 years or when you see roots growing out of the drainage holes. Choose a pot that is 1-2 inches larger in diameter. Spring is the best time for repotting.
